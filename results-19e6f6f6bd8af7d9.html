What you're looking at. Everyone was asked to rank layouts in two separate groups, because a ceiling beam splits the hall into two rooms and the six tables can be arranged two ways:
• “4 in one room, 2 in the other” — four tables clustered in one half, a pair in the other (the “Four On Top” family).
• “3 in each room” — the tables in a single line, three per half (management may prefer this split, so it's ranked separately).
Each person gave a 1st and (optional) 2nd choice in each group. Below, each group is tallied on its own.
