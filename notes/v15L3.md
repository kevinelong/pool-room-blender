# v15L3 — Cue realistic-rest geometry

## Motivation
Cues were previously drawn as horizontal axis-aligned boxes at Z = 32.5" (just
above the felt). This meant the entire 58" cue sat AT rail height and appeared
to intersect the rail edge, and the butt end floated over the service lane.
User asked to:
1. Move each cue 1 ft (12") closer to its pool table
2. Tip must **touch** the table
3. Butt must sit **above** the rail (not intersect it)

## Chosen interpretation (user-selected)
"Realistic natural rest: butt just above rail (~38"), gentle tilt" — keeps the
full 58" cue length, shifts the whole cue 12" inward (tip deeper on felt, butt
correspondingly 12" less far into the aisle), and adds a 6° tilt so the butt
end sits 6" above the tip end.

## Geometry
| Parameter | Before (v15L2) | After (v15L3) |
|---|---|---|
| Tip Z | 32.5" | **32.0"** (bed top) |
| Butt Z | 32.5" | **38.0"** (2.5" above 35.5" rail top) |
| Inset from rail (tip depth onto felt) | 4" | **16"** (shifted 12" closer) |
| Horizontal run tip→butt | 58" | **57.69"** (from 58² - 6²) |
| Tilt | 0° | **~5.9°** upward from tip to butt |
| Mesh type | axis-aligned boxes | **rotated tapered cylinders** (16 verts, smooth) |
| Shaft radius | 0.30" (box, tip end) | **0.30"** tip → **0.475"** at 2/3 split |
| Butt radius | 0.625" (box, butt end) | **0.475"** at split → **0.625"** butt end |

## Server-clearance analysis (informational)
West side (mirror on east):
- Service lane runs x = 18" (two-top east edge) to x = 78.25" (west rail) = **60.25"** wide
- Cue butt sits at **x = 36.6", z = 38"**
- Horizontal gap between butt and two-top / bar stools: **18.6"**
- Horizontal gap between butt and rail: **41.7"**
- Cue Y-footprint is a single ~1.25"-thick line at the table's mid-length; a
  server can sidestep 12"+ north or south to get the full 60.25" clearance
- Butt sits at hip-to-thigh height for a 5'4"–5'10" adult (38" ≤ typical hip 39–43")

If a future revision wants under-cue tray clearance for a server walking through
without sidestepping, raise CUE_BUTT_Z to 42–44" (tilt ~12–16°).
